Towns & Countries

# Introduction

Towns and Countries (called “States” for roleplay purposes) are settlements that can be founded by anyone once all requirements to do so are met. You don’t need permission to found either, but you do need to inform the Game Masters when you intend to do so.

On this server both serve as administrative units, vital parts of our government system. Founding either hence comes not only with privileges, but also with responsibilities and limitations outlined in the Constitution of the Federation of White Tiger.

The Federation of White Tiger is only competent for all government matters where it has exclusive influence. Where a town or country also has influence, the Federation is only competent for its exclusive federal responsibilities and beyond that may only act as a substitute for a town or country if and insofar that fails to fulfil its own responsibilities.

# Founding a Settlement

Founding a town requires 5 players willing to join it in a radius of 125 metres around the Town Foundation, whereas founding a country requires 15 players and at least one town willing to join it in a radius of 300 metres around the Country Foundation.

Note: The name of the elected title for the head of government of the settlement must be “Mayor” for towns and “Governor” for countries and should include the name of the settlement. This ensures easy recognizability of the important titles. Private properties and institutions existing in your settlement should not be mistakeable for governmental ones, preferably by ensuring that the settlement name is only used by your government.

To place a settlement foundation, follow this list:

1. Craft a Town Foundation or Country Foundation at a Settlement Crafting Table.
2. Place the respective foundation on the central claim of where you want the town or country to emit its influence from. A Town Foundation must be in a basic T1, a Country Foundation in a basic T2 room.
3. You will now be considered the first Mayor and your settlement will automatically be part of the Federation of White Tiger.
4. Open a [ticket on Discord](https://www.google.com/url?q=https://discord.com/channels/254025510651297802/1343455490700607560&sa=D&source=editors&ust=1776691474436295&usg=AOvVaw3Twerz3RNdtC_94XhEN9NK) with the coordinates of your foundation deed for it to be added to the “Administrative Units” District Map and to receive a grant of 1.000 € (2.500 € for countries) paid into its treasury from the Federal Bank.

Once a Game Master informs you about that having been done, follow this list:

1. Expand the foundation deed to at least cover the building it is contained in and check the status tab of the foundation on if it is active.
2. In the foundation tab, name your settlement.
3. Invite players or have them apply and approve them in the citizens tab.
4. Draft a constitution.
5. Once the settlement has the required number of citizens joined, start the ratification of the constitution to create an election draft.
6. Add anything else you want to the election draft, such as laws or districts.
7. Put the election draft up for vote.
8. Once the ratification vote ends successfully, the settlement is founded and its influence area becomes active.

Mayors and Governors can request Game Masters to receive a chat tag.

# Expanding a Settlement

Towns start with an influence range of 125 metres that can extend up to 200 metres whereas Countries start with an influence range of 300 metres that can extend up to 600 metres.

To extend the influence range you need to improve the culture value on the foundation deed, for each 25 (for towns) / 75 (for countries) culture beyond the minimum you gain 50 % towards the maximum influence range.

You can also use cultural properties to extend influence in a more directed way. These properties emit their own influence range, hence it is best to place it near a border at a location where you want to extend the border. A cultural property follows the same rules as a foundation deed but does not start with an influence range without requiring culture. Towns can build up to 30 and countries can build up to 100 cultural properties.

Please note that using cultural properties in unintended ways, resulting in otherwise impossible influence, is considered an exploit violating § 2 (1) of our server rules.

Additionally, due to the idea of settlements on this server being administrative units in the same Federation, cultural properties should always be properties that actually contain culturally valuable buildings and should never be used to fight about influence with neighboring settlements - such behaviour may violate the constitution and could be considered malfeasance. Instead, once borders of settlements start to clash, a mutual agreement on permanent borders should be made. Using the open borders feature can additionally help players living at a border with small, unavoidable changes to influence.

# Annexing as a Settlement

The server concept intends that, in the optimal case, every location in the world is covered by the influence of both a town and a country additional to the Federation. Settlements are hence encouraged to annex homesteads that are not yet members of a settlement, especially when the settlement not only borders but surrounds a homestead - this ensures that settlement territory is coherent and the homestead can be expanded.

Please pay attention when annexing though, as the game systems may also allow you to annex homesteads that are already members of a neighboring settlement - the constitution however does not allow you to annex these without the consent of both the player owning the homestead and the settlements it is part of.

Consequently, countries may also freely annex towns, though talking with the town to be annexed beforehands is always a good idea. Please note that annexing a town doesn’t make them a “vassal”, it still enjoys all rights the constitution grants to it and if it is disputed which country the town should be a member of, the Senate may decide finally.

Players owning a homestead located in the influence of a settlement are always entitled to citizenship of it. Players that do not wish to live in a specific settlement can leave it by moving their homestead away from the settlement. If organized moving isn’t feasible, as a last resort, players can use the /GetHomeSteadBack command, which will instantly unclaim the homestead deed and give the homestead back to place it elsewhere.

# Playing in a Settlement

It is important to understand that the purpose of settlements on White Tiger is not to avoid undesired legislation or enable collaboration of players - if you want to do that, you are still required to found a company.

The purpose of settlements on White Tiger is for them to be player-founded local government authorities tasked with fulfilling public functions, as such their property must be strictly separated from private property and all governmental projects and responsibilities must be financed by settlement funds. Sharing private and settlement funds or property is a violation of § 2 (4) of our server rules. The only exception to this is the initial foundation process, where private property may be used to create a basic room and the foundation that needs to be placed into it.

In recognition of how the game is typically played it is tolerated when players build public infrastructure on unclaimed land on their own, despite that being a typical governmental responsibility. Settlements are hence not required to reimburse the goods used or compensate for the work done for public infrastructure that was established in this way, but that does only apply as long as the settlement is not involved in any way. That means that once the settlement or an official of it does participate in creation of any specific part of public infrastructure, even if only via coordination or guidance, it becomes a governmental project that must be financed by settlement funds. Failing to do so is considered collaboration, and hence a violation of § 2 (4) of our server rules.

Settlements must follow the Constitution of the Federation of White Tiger and all legislation of settlements they are a member of - as such their constitutions must fulfil basic democratic requirements and they need to meet any responsibilities delegated to them by parent settlements. They are required to buy all goods needed in official stores passively and to fairly compensate players that work for them either through contracts or titles with wages. The latter does not apply to holders of an Elected Title, but we strongly encourage settlements to pay their officials wages like the Federation of White Tiger does, though at this point this is not yet a requirement.

Settlements are intended to fund themselves through means that are typical for governments and like the Federation hence may generally not accept donations. For solely cultural projects however, players may donate to the federal Minister of Culture who can distribute such funds to settlements while supervising their usage. He may on request at his own discretion also create separate accounts for specific projects or settlements, allowing donations to be made that are appropriated to a specific purpose.

# Legislating in a Settlement

While the civics system should prevent anything that players aren’t supposed to be able to do, it may - due to its high complexity - still be possible to do unintended things. You are hence responsible to ensure that all your legislation, be it a law or executive action, only affects actions and players that are under your settlement’s jurisdiction. An action is considered under a settlement’s jurisdiction if it happens in its influence and a player is considered under a settlement’s jurisdiction if they are a citizen of that settlement.

Legislation that affects actions or players not under a settlement’s jurisdiction is void. Intentionally drafting such legislation or failing to immediately (once noticed) inform Game Masters about such legislation and remedy its effects is a violation of § 2 (1) of our server rules.

# Banners / Flags for Settlements

To incentivize players to found recurring settlements and to promote identity with them, we implemented Banner and Flag objects that can display a selectable flag on them.  
  
To get a flag for your settlement added to the selection, you need to provide two graphic files (.png recommended, but any common format should work) with the dimensions listed below that contain your flag image and send them in a ticket to the Game Masters on our Discord. The flag does not need to be professional, but should in some way relate to your settlement, be at least similar for both dimensions and may not violate server rules. It will be added to the selection as soon as possible after it has been approved.

If you would like a flag but aren’t able to create one yourself, you can open a ticket as well. Game Master Sunlight has volunteered to create flags, but please note that she does so in her free time and that it is solely up to her on if and when she creates one.  
  
Dimensions for Banner object: 512 x 1024 px (Note that its bottom will partly be cut off)

Dimensions for Flag object: 1920 x 1200 px

Flags should be presented at every government building, either near to its main entrance or at a suitable place on top of it. The order of the flags when viewed from their front should be Federation, (Parent) Country, (Member) Town(s) from left to right, settlements that don’t have a flag are skipped. Presenting the flags correctly may be rewarded in the Culture Contest.
