Government Guidelines

# Introduction

This document describes guidelines that apply to the governments of all settlements and the Federation.

# Legislation Requirements

1. Taxation must always use “Smart Tax” - this ensures that taxes that couldn't be paid by a player aren’t waived but instead being remembered for later collection while the player also does not get prevented from the action. It also ensures sufficient reporting for investigating issues with taxation.
2. Taxation on wealth acquired through the constitutional UBI is prohibited. This means that wealth taxes must have a 0% bracket of at least (UBI per hour  \* world age in hours) and income taxes must have a 0% bracket of at least the UBI payments over its duration of collection. (Example: An income tax acting every 3 hours must have a 0% bracket of 3 \* UBI per hour.)
3. Taxation on companies must be adjusted to only target income they get to keep after the company tax that applies to them due to our server rules. You can do so by multiplication of the desired value you want to tax with “percent” the “companyTaxRate” custom stat.
4. Taxation has to respect rebates when both happen from the same government account, so the balances are cleared and notifications are kept to a minimum.

Game Masters may correct or remove legislation not fulfilling these requirements.

# Public Infrastructure

Governments are responsible for all public infrastructure within their influence that they are supposed to manage, no matter if it has been built as part of governmental policy or by citizens on their own. In return, such infrastructure is protected by Federal Law.

## Types of Public Infrastructure

Public infrastructure on land is separated into the following types:

1. "Federal Highways", that consist of predefined roads spanning the world and all roads connecting states with each other and the federal capital. They are managed by the federal government and have the highest building priority.
2. "State Routes", which are all roads connecting towns and other locations within states to each other. They are managed by the respective state governments.
3. "County Roads", which are all roads within towns that do not fall into one of the road types listed above. They are managed by the respective town governments.

Public infrastructure on water is separated into the following types:

1. “Waterways”, which are all bodies of water within the biomes “ocean” and “deep ocean”. They are managed by the federal government.
2. "Rivers", which are all natural, navigable bodies of water not considered a waterway. They are managed by the lowest local authority applicable.
3. “Canals”, which are all bodies of water not considered a waterway and artificially created or expanded. They are managed by the lowest local authority applicable.

## Requirements for Public Infrastructure

1. Constructions must be physically realistic and comparable to their counterparts in real life.
2. Roads must have an unobstructed minimum roadway height and width of five blocks. They should avoid changes in height level over their course where aesthetically and realistically possible and not be built below height 140 for flood control. It is recommended for all roads of a specific type to have a unified design in a region. Federal Highways and State Routes specifically must fit symmetrically into an even number of claims.
3. Tunnels must have coating for its ceiling and walls made from realistic materials.
4. Bridges must have realistic safety and support structures. Where they cross waterways, they must in at least one and should where possible in at least two central locations have an unobstructed space of at least 11 metres in height above water level and 10 metres in width below them. Where they cross canals or rivers they must at least allow single lane traffic for Wooden Transport Boats and provide a waiting area when parallel passing of such is not possible.
5. Bridges, entries to rivers that are crossed by a bridge and entries to canals must be signalled with signs on which types of watercraft can pass them and in what lane configuration.

Game Masters may cause artificial disasters that damage, destroy or impact the usability of public infrastructure listed above when it doesn’t follow these requirements. They may only do so starting on Day 7 and only after a public warning about a disaster potentially occurring at or with the suspected location has been given, allowing governments sufficient time to take emergency measures to prevent damage.
