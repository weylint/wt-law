# Education & Skill Scrolls

The Minister of Education (MoE) is an appointed federal government position which is charged with purchasing research and developing the technical skills of the Federation's citizens. Skill Books are centrally crafted by the MoE at the Federal University, located in Unica, and skill scrolls are sold to the citizens. State Colleges and Town Schools can assist the MoE in selling some skill scrolls.

Research generally follows a schedule, however depending on research availability some skills may be released later. Government policy could also add additional requirements to release skill scrolls to the public.

Important Note: Skill Scrolls do NOT give claim papers.

## Town Schools

A town may elect to construct a school for its citizens convenience. The mayor or citizens should appoint a School Teacher. The Mayor (or designee) should notify the Minister of Education to district the school and assign the School Teacher title.

The teacher can locally craft skill scrolls for the following skills:

- Farming

- Butchery

- Masonry

- Carpentry

- Shipwright

- Basic Engineering

- Tailoring

- Milling

- Smelting

- Fertilizers

- Blacksmith

- Pottery

- Glassworking

- Painting

- Cooking

- Baking

More advanced skills must be learned at a State College or Federal University.

The teacher receives a wage based on the server's index wage from the Federal wage budget. The town may request funding from the federal government, but should have its own source of revenue to sustain necessary services.

## State Colleges

A State may elect to construct a college for its citizens's convenience. The Governor or citizens should appoint a College Professor. The Governor (or designee) should notify the Minister of Education to district the college and assign the College Professor title.

The professor can locally craft skill scrolls for the following skills in addition to those available at Town Schools:

- Mechanics

- Paper Milling

- Advanced Smelting

- Advanced Cooking

- Advanced Baking

More advanced skills must be learned at the Federal University.

The professor receives a wage based on the server's index wage from the Federal Wage budget. The State may request funding from the federal government, but should have its own source of revenue to sustain necessary services.
