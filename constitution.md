# § 1 The Federation

(1) <sup>1</sup>The Federation of White Tiger is a democratic and social federal republic composed of countries - referred to as states - and towns, that both can be founded freely by a sufficient number of citizens. <sup>2</sup>The federation, states and towns are administrative units in descending hierarchical order. <sup>3</sup>Towns may become members of states by elective accession or mandatory annexation, with the Senate deciding finally in case of disputes.

(2) <sup>1</sup>Advancement of the federation and environment protection are national objectives.

(3) <sup>1</sup>The legislature, the executive and the judiciary shall be bound by this constitution.

(4) <sup>1</sup>The elections for all federal public offices shall be held on Days 1, 8, 15, 22 and 29.

(5) <sup>1</sup>Retired officials continue to serve in acting capacity until the successor takes office.

# § 2 Basic Rights

(1) <sup>1</sup>All citizens of the federation are equal before the law. <sup>2</sup>They enjoy freedom of faith, conscience and ideological confession as well as freedom of movement and residence. <sup>3</sup>They have the right to - in the boundaries of law - assemble peacefully, freely choose their occupation, freely express their opinion and form societies and other associations.

(2) <sup>1</sup>Minorities and ethical playstyles may not be discriminated against, legislation that would disproportionately affect them must provide for appropriate hardship provisions.

<sup>2</sup>Settlements must treat all their residents equally and may not limit or tax access to and usage of public property based on citizenship or residence. <sup>3</sup>They may however levy reasonable fees or taxes from non-residents for access to and usage of public services.

(3) <sup>1</sup>Private property shall be guaranteed, but it entails obligations and its use shall also serve the public good. <sup>2</sup>Expropriation is only permissible for the public good and in conjunction with equitable compensation. <sup>3</sup>The Federal Bank may hold bank deposits of abandoned citizens of the federation in trust until their return.

(4) <sup>1</sup>Taxes on income may exclusively be levied by the Federation in an equitable, progressive form and not surpass 50% of the taxable income. <sup>2</sup>Taxes on the trade of goods may exclusively be levied by states and towns and not surpass 5% of the transaction value per administrative unit; this does not apply to steering taxes.<sup>3</sup>Citizens that haven’t learned a specialty may not be subject to taxes on the trade of goods at stores they don’t own and expenses related to such trades must be considered fully when determining their taxable income.  
(5) <sup>1</sup>Government agencies are required to purchase all goods necessary to fulfil their duties passively at official stores and may only actively purchase goods at private stores in cases of justified necessity. <sup>2</sup>Unless explicitly stated otherwise, they may not accept donations. <sup>3</sup>Unauthorized acceptance of donations constitutes malfeasance.

# § 3 States and Towns

(1) <sup>1</sup>States and Towns enjoy autonomy for their finances, governance and legislation - only limited by the legislation of any administrative unit they are member of, which takes precedence when in conflict with local legislation if deviation is not explicitly permitted. <sup>2</sup>They freely determine the structure of their constitution within the limits of this paragraph. <sup>3</sup>Their non-abandoned citizens may at any time start or vote in a referendum to amend its constitution, the result being determined by simple majority after 24 hours.

(2) <sup>1</sup>The constitution of each State and Town must establish a Governor respectively Mayor as Head of Government that must be electable by all non-abandoned citizens of the respective state or town from all active citizens of the same. <sup>2</sup>The legislature of a state or town is performed by its head of government, except for if its constitution determines it to instead be performed by specific bodies to which no executive powers may then be delegated. <sup>3</sup>The jurisprudence is always performed by the federal judiciary.

(3) <sup>1</sup>Constitutional powers may not be bound directly to specific citizens. <sup>2</sup>Citizens of a state or town may demand an election to be held for an office they are eligible to vote for if that office is vacant or the last election for it finished more than 6 days ago. <sup>3</sup>State officials may not hold an office in the Federation or any other State during their term.

(4) <sup>1</sup>States and Towns develop their territory, primarily by establishing infrastructure and public services and support the Federation with its duties. <sup>2</sup>Their executive may appoint Civil Servants serving at its pleasure or instruct contractors to assist them in doing so, in exchange for adequate compensation. <sup>3</sup>They may assign property and zone their territory at their political discretion, exercised in a reasonable way free of arbitrariness.  
(5) <sup>1</sup>The State of Unica serving as the Capital City of the Federation additionally has the obligation to provide a central living, meeting and trade hub as well as a place to house the buildings for the constitutional institutions and federal public services. <sup>2</sup>It is entitled to reasonable financial support from the Federation to fulfil these additional obligations.

# § 4 The Congress

(1) <sup>1</sup>The Congress is composed of The House of Representatives (abbreviated as “The House”) as its lower and The Senate as its upper chamber. <sup>2</sup>The House consists of the Prime Minister and 4 Members of Parliament that shall be elected by all non-abandoned citizens of the Federation from the active citizens of the same in a single election for a legislative period of 7 days with no term limit applying, vacancies shall be filled until the next election by eligible candidates defeated in the last election in order of the election result. <sup>3</sup>The Senate consists of the Heads of Government of each State as Senators.

(2) <sup>1</sup>The House exercises the fiscal sovereignty of the Federation, providing the Federal Government with budgets and regulations on their use and is responsible to enact legislation in accordance with constitutional law to fulfil the national objectives and to provide policy for a healthy cohabitation of the society.<sup>2</sup>Its members shall be paid a wage according to the federal salary scale and enjoy immunity from criminal prosecution during their tenure that can only be lifted by a simple majority vote of the House. <sup>3</sup>If the House is equally divided, the Prime Minister casts the tie-breaking vote. <sup>4</sup>It may conduct public investigations and for that purpose has the authority to require production of evidence and testimony. <sup>5</sup>It may authorize government agencies to act as a market competitor where that is reasonable to restore or preserve economic stability.

(3) <sup>1</sup>The Senate has the authority to veto the appointment of Federal Ministers and State Secretaries within 24 hours of their appointment, but such veto may be overridden by a two thirds majority in the House. <sup>2</sup>Federal legislation incurring debt to the Federal Bank requires the consent of the Senate to be valid, which may not be granted if the resulting debt would exceed 10% of the currency in circulation that was not created by debts. <sup>3</sup>Federal legislation explicitly affecting specific states or their territory requires the consent by the affected states or the Senate to be valid.

# § 5 The Federal President

(1) <sup>1</sup>The Federal President is the Head of State and as such enjoys sovereign immunity, the office is exercised by the Community Manager of Eco. <sup>2</sup>He wields the power to veto federal legislation within 24 hours of its passage and all federal text laws require his signature to be valid. <sup>3</sup>He may, on petition of any citizen and with advice of the House, order referendums to be held, them being binding if they were announced to be and at least 2,5 % of all citizens voted while 10 % of the population was still active at their start.

(2) <sup>1</sup>He may, on request of the Prime Minister or if a vacancy of that office cannot be filled, dissolve the House. <sup>2</sup>When the House is dissolved, the current legislative period ends prematurely with all consequences that entails and an immediate re-election of the House is held. <sup>3</sup>The resulting legislative period only lasts until the next general election.

(3) <sup>1</sup>He supervises the Federal Bank and may appoint a Director of the Federal Bank as a State Secretary serving at his pleasure that has the authority to establish and enforce monetary policy regarding the currency “Euro” as the sole legal tender of the Federation.

# § 6 The Federal Government

(1) <sup>1</sup>The Federal Government consists of the Prime Minister; the Federal Ministers of Culture, Education and Infrastructure as well as any number of State Secretaries managing government departments not established in this constitution, which shall all be paid a salary according to the federal salary scale. <sup>2</sup>The Prime Minister shall be elected by all non-abandoned citizens of the Federation from the active citizens of the same for the next legislative period with no term limit applying. <sup>3</sup>The Federal Ministers and State Secretaries are appointed by the Prime Minister from the active citizens of the Federation for the current legislative period with no term limit applying and serve at the pleasure of the Prime Minister. <sup>4</sup>As long as their appointment can be vetoed, they serve in acting capacity. <sup>5</sup>All members of the Federal Government may adjust the coverage of established districts related to their duties in accordance with their stated purpose.

(2) ¹The Prime Minister is the head of government, determines the guidelines of government policy and has the authority to issue government bonds at his own discretion. <sup>2</sup>If the office becomes vacant, the first willing, incumbent Minister in order of department creation becomes Prime Minister for the remainder of the legislative period.

(3) <sup>1</sup>The Minister of Education is responsible to acquire “Research Papers” to research technologies via “Skill Books” at the respective federal facility and makes those available by crafting "Skill Scrolls" and distributing them in an equitable manner. <sup>2</sup>The federal education system should be managed economically and must receive funding from the House necessary to guarantee smooth operations ensuring the scientific progression of the country according to the concept schedule through organisation of federation-wide efforts.

(4) <sup>1</sup>The Minister of Infrastructure oversees and supports the development of the global electricity network, the Federal Highways and the Waterways, including public ports. <sup>2</sup>He advises the house on related budget plans and construction programs, determines the priority of construction projects and works with all local authorities to ensure their obligations in regard to local infrastructure are fulfilled and supported where necessary.

(5) <sup>1</sup>The Minister of Culture is responsible for the cultural development of the Federation as a whole, collaborating closely with its states and towns. <sup>2</sup>He encourages and supports culturally valuable buildings, events and other projects and plans and executes such on behalf of the Federation. <sup>3</sup>Additionally to his budget he may accept donations to a governmental bank account, which may only be used to fulfill the duties of his office.

(6) <sup>1</sup>The Federal Government as a whole ensures the execution of federal legislation. <sup>2</sup>It develops the Federation, primarily by establishing infrastructure and public services in all of its territory. <sup>3</sup>It may appoint Federal Civil Servants serving at its pleasure or instruct adequately compensated Federal Contractors to assist it in doing so.

# § 7 The Judiciary

(1) <sup>1</sup>The Judiciary consists of Magistrate Judges which are Federal Civil Servants appointed by and serving at the pleasure of the Chief Justice, Game Masters acting as Federal Judges and the Community Manager of Eco acting as Chief Justice.

(2) <sup>1</sup>Any citizen with standing may file a lawsuit by providing the claim and all evidence backing it in federal court. <sup>2</sup>If the lawsuit is an appeal against a decision by a Magistrate or Federal Judge, a dispute based on constitutional law or an impeachment of public officials it is to be petitioned to the Chief Justice for acceptance and final decision.

# § 8 Sovereign Acts

(1) <sup>1</sup>Legislation must be sufficiently determined, equitable and proportionate as well as suitable and necessary to reach the goal that is pursued with it. <sup>2</sup>Its enforcement is incumbent upon the lowest competent administrative unit if not explicitly stated otherwise. <sup>3</sup>Insofar it is incapable or unwilling to fulfil this duty, any higher competent administrative unit may execute substitute performance and, with consent of the Federal President, may issue binding orders, withhold financial resources or temporarily assume control over the administrative units' government to enforce compliance.

(2) <sup>1</sup>Administrative acts are the imposition of any sovereign measure by the executive of a competent administrative unit to further a legitimate end and must be suitable, necessary and proportionate. <sup>2</sup>They must be notified to those concerned in a manner that ensures its reception and provide information about the measure imposed, the date it was imposed, its reasons, any costs that must be borne by the recipient and an instruction on the right of appeal within three days of their issuance. <sup>3</sup>Measures involving access to or alteration of real estate and confiscation or expropriation of property require prior judicial approval, unless the measure is taken due to imminent danger or as a distress for unpaid debt or fines. <sup>4</sup>Administrative fines may be imposed for violation of laws that provide this option within the range determined by them. <sup>5</sup>They may be doubled if the violation is either intentional, persistent after 24 hours or a repeat offence and the then resulting fine must be doubled if the offender is a legal entity.

(3) <sup>1</sup>Justice is administered according to procedures resembling an inquisitorial, civic law legal system. <sup>2</sup>The Judiciary may order any suitable and proportionate measure necessary to redress ascertained injustice provided that applied law does not provide an exhaustive list of legal consequences or requires specific ones. <sup>3</sup>In criminal cases they may impose fines measured in daily rates multiplicated by half of the applicable average daily income, the temporary or permanent forfeiture of rights and temporary or permanent imprisonment depending on which punishment the violated law specifies and with all these legal consequences except permanent imprisonment being issuable in a suspended form and intended to regularly be issued in such form for a first-time offence. <sup>4</sup>For minor offences they may issue a warning instead of a penalty or convert a fine to community service of one hour per incurred daily rate if the convict requests that. <sup>5</sup>Injunctions are reserved for the judiciary and may not be used by anyone not part of it.

# § 9 Education System

(1) <sup>1</sup>The federal education system consists of Lower Education provided from Day 1, Higher Education provided from Day 8 and Greater Education provided from Day 15 through the Federal University.

(2) <sup>1</sup>Skill scrolls may also be distributed locally in towns by School Teachers in School House districts for Lower Education and in states by College Professors in State College districts for Higher Education. <sup>2</sup>School Teachers and College Professors are Federal Civil Servants appointed by and serving at the pleasure of their respective local government, requiring confirmation by the Minister of Education.

# § 10 Special Regulations

(1) <sup>1</sup>Deviating from §2 (4) taxes on income may have brackets with a taxation exceeding 50% of the taxable income, as long as those brackets only target players with an income value that compared to the income value of most other taxed entities is a significant outlier. <sup>2</sup>Taxation resulting from documented components of a tax on income that serve the purpose of altering the taxation based on vertical integration does not count against the limitation.
